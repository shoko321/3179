WORLD HAPPINESS

From 2015 to 2022

The World Happiness Report assesses global happiness levels by considering factors like economic prosperity, social support, health, freedom of choice, trust, and generosity. A higher ranking in the report indicates greater overall happiness in a country. This report goes beyond material wealth, emphasizing the importance of mental well-being, social connections, and community resilience.



GLOBAL HAPPINESS OVERVIEW

This map depicts the happiness score categories of countries worldwide. Over the years, North American, European, and Australian countries consistently demonstrate a very high happiness score. In contrast, African, South Asian, and West Asian countries consistently exhibit a very low happiness score.

Factors

GDP per Capita: The average income of a country's residents and serves as an indicator of their economic well-being.

Social Support: It measures individuals' reliance on their social networks, friends, and family for assistance and emotional connections in society.

Health: Health encompress the well-being and physical condition of a population, including life expectancy, healthcare access, and disease prevalence.

Freedom: Freedom measures personal and political liberties, including expression, assembly, and choice in a society.

Government Trust: Government trust reflects citizen confidence in government institutions, indicating transparency and accountability.

Generosity: Generosity measures individuals' willingness to help and donate, reflecting social responsibility.
